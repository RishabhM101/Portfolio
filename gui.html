Satellite Telemetry Display

Numbers can tell a story, but a visual representation brings the data to life

[Touch and scroll to rotate]

Quaternions:

During my internship at Space Technology and Aeronautical Rocketry, I used 3D visualization to display the orientation of our satellite in real-time, providing valuable insights for the ground station.

To achieve this, I utilized the MPU6050 sensor and RF 433MHz modules for data transmission and reception. By implementing a complementary filter on the MPU6050's gyroscope and accelerometer data I was able to calculate roll, pitch, and yaw. However, I faced some challenges, such as gimbal lock and yaw drift, using this process.

Quaternions to the rescue:

Working with quaternions can be a unique experience that presents both challenges and rewards. Though the concept of quaternions may initially be confusing, their practical application can be easy to work with. Their ability to represent rotations in 3D space without the limitations of gimbal lock makes it a powerful tool to work with.
